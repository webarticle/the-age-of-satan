May 20, 2043, morning

From the guide’s speech in the Museum of Archaeology:

"And this, ladies and gentlemen, is the biggest mystery of all times. This object here was found only recently, some ten years ago. It looks like a wristwatch, but actually it is a message left for us. If you look closely, you’ll see the rough back surface. This is actually writing, in contemporary world language, which can be read only under microscope. Here’s the message:

‘Beware of the power contained in this device. The Evil of our planet is locked within. We know that you’ll be reasonable enough as a race (even though you’ll be so different from us) and this is why we entrust to you with the role of Guardians of the Evil. The Evil should be guarded with utmost care. If somebody puts this watch on his wrist, the Evil shall be free and the Age of Satan will set in. Should you allow this to happen, your only chance shall be the liquid in the small bottle you will find together with the device. The Evil should be sprinkled with it while it’s second day at large has not run out or you’ll never be able to deal with it. Humankind shall be erased from Earth.’

"And, do you know what the strangest things about this wrist watch are? First, all analyses show that it can be dated back some thirty thousand years before Christ. That means that the creatures who left it here could see far ahead into the future. Second, the device shows the time with perfect precision in all time-zones it is moved across. It moves an hour ahead or back exactly when it is taken across the meridian. And it is hollow. There’s nothing inside this box you see here. No mechanism, simple or complex, just nothing. Third, it is made of some unknown metal. Scientists are positive that there’s no such metal on Earth. It’s a pity we lived in caves when our planet was visited by creatures of higher intelligence. Some scientists think that indeed there is something to this thing here, while others shrug it off, saying somebody is trying to find out whether we are naive enough to waste our time on it. I personally am more inclined to believe the latter, but that’s immaterial.

May 21, 2043, 4 PM

"How kind of the guides to tell you all you need to make a good plan!"

Ed Harris was a petty thief and had never thought of stealing anything bigger than a lady’s purse. However, after his yesterday’s tour around the Archaeological Museum, he was overwhelmed by desire for power. If he could only lay his hands on this "watch" or whatever it was! He could then rule over the entire world! Of course, it might be that those extraterrestrial palls had pulled a big joke and the inscription on the back was nothing but rubbish. But Ed believed the massage was true. He hoped others believed, too, or otherwise everything was just a heap of trash. Anyway, he could try. If he failed... well, he knew what life in the can looked like. He had nothing to lose, so he decided to give it a try.

May 21, 2043, midnight

The wire cut into the flesh of his throat at once. Jerry tried to scream, to warn his partner, but in vain. It had to be admitted, the one who was trying to kill him, whoever he was, knew his job well. After a few fruitless attempts to break free, the uniformed guard went numb. He still felt the wire cutting through his flesh, severing muscles and tendons. The last thing he was aware of, before departing on his last journey, was the relaxation of his vocal chords, when this most easily handled and perhaps most deadly of the murder accessories went through them.

Ed relieved his hold and gently lay the dead guard’s body on the floor. Now he was playing in a higher class - he was now a murderer! And he had done wonderfully for a debut. The man couldn’t make a sound before he died. Now the second guard remained. The thief propped the dead body against the wall in order to gain some time. Then sneaked to the glass-case where the "watch" was.

Matthew smiled slightly. Jerry was again dozing off. He knew he had to wake him and went closer to shake him up. He felt sorry for his partner. The man was working too much - besides the night shifts in the museum, he also had a day job. Anyway, if the boss got wind Jerry was sleeping when on duty, he’d fire him at once. Matthew tapped his shoulder expecting to startle him as usual. This time however "napping" Jerry slid sideways and fell on the floor, revealing the slit in his throat. Matthew stifled his scream. Jerry’s murderer was still around.

"Who’s there? Show me your mug before I find you or you’ll come to grief!" Matthew wasn’t all that sure he could do anything make his threat a good one. This was the first corpse he had ever seen and he felt like going to the men’s room and throwing up everything he had eaten during the day. But he was a guard. He had no right to do that.

"All right, listen to me, buddy, unless you want to follow your friend here." The voice seemed to come from all directions. Matthew looked around, scared. "Now you tell me the magic code that opens this showcase and I leave you to live your miserable life to its natural end, deal?"

The last grain of courage left Matthew.

"Please, don’t kill me! I’ll tell you all you want!"

"Fine. I’m listening."

"‘Evil’. The code is ‘evil’."

"Good. Now let’s see if that’s true. If the alarm goes off you’re a dead man, okay?" He paused. "Good boys never lie. Sleep tight!"

A few seconds later Matthew joined his partner in the afterlife. The last thing that crossed his mind was that one should never trust criminals.

Ed Harris took the "watch" and disappeared into the unsuspecting night.

May 23, 2043, 1 PM

"If you don’t give me these five million, he would be obsessed by Evil of unknown proportions, don’t you understand?" Ed was trying hard to squeeze some profit out of his new acquisition. Most people, however, were skeptical and did not mind to put the watch on. His plan was gradually falling to pieces. The police had no idea who had killed the two guards and so far he was getting away with it. He was sick and tired of failures and this time had decided to stick to his threat. If they refused to pay him, this time he was definitely going to put the watch on this rich bastard’s wrist, pretentious and phony as he was. He kept trying with the rich, because they never called the police. Most of them had some dark secret they didn’t want advertised and preferred to stay low. His theory had worked right so far - nobody had called the police. But no one was willing to pay either. They didn’t seem to give a damn whether he put the lousy watch on their wrists or not.

Well, it was high time for this wretch to pay for the stupidity of the rest of them. He had kidnapped nine people in the last two days, and had turned them free again, because they had no intention to pay. They would have a good story of kidnapping to tell their grandchildren, but Ed’s patience had run out.

"Fine. Don’t tell me I didn’t warn you!" He switched off the purring machine, which he called that because it purred like a cat. It was actually a device which could control, by voice commands, what the other end of the line got on their communication screens - picture, brightness, contrast, sound volume and quality, and so on. He could change his voice and remove himself from the picture, while leaving the rest of the interior intact. Now he had done just that and his voice was so scary, that he himself shuddered.

Ed Harris went to his hostage, trying hard to look tough and dangerous. In fact he was afraid nothing would come out of it. Overcoming his doubts and fears, he took his victim’s hand and clicked the watch in place. The man’s name was Gary Trenton.

"This watch is v-v-very n-n-ice," Gary began, but his words died down.

Ed watched in horror the transformation the guy was undergoing. Under his shirt, on his belly, two lumps began protruding, seemingly ready to burst out. His arms split along their length with a disgusting swash! and suddenly he had four arms. Then his eyes began bubbling and dividing into smaller globules until, finally, they looked like the eyes of an insect. The lumps on his belly burst out of his shirt and two repugnant, snake-like tentacles, red and slimy, covered with suckers, began slithering around. They had something like dark-bluish tripled tongues which darted forward and back all of the time. The creature’s skin became poison green, fissures formed, then the fissures became scales. The spikes growing on his back ripped through his clothes. His mouth shrunk to a red spot and then thrust forth like a proboscis.

"T-t-thanks," the creature said with an effort.

So he could speak! Ed thought of what might happen if he caught this... thing, and started showing him to people for money. A lot of money! Carried away by his dreams, he hardly felt the slight pricking in his eye. A moment later he was wriggling on the floor in terrible pain. The last five minutes of his life were an unbearable agony. When, at last, death came, he felt relieved.

"Thanks," the monster said, "for having released me."

And freedom was sweet! After all this time in the detestable watch! He lifted his foot and crushed the device in one single blow. Then he dispersed his molecules into the air, mustering his strength for his fateful second day on this planet.

May 23, 2043, the five o’clock news

"Millionaire Gary Trenton has reportedly disappeared today..." The face of a fair-haired young man in his thirties appeared on the screen. Dark eyes, feminine features. His height, weight and other distinctions were typed below. "If you have some information which could be of help to establish his whereabouts, please call 245 169 875. Thank you. Earlier today, the dead body of Mr. Ed Harris was found in his apartment on 62 Everest street. Mr. Harris had a criminal record and had served several short term sentences for petty theft. Experts say that death has been inflicted by some unknown, very powerful poison, causing almost instant, but also very painful death. The poison was delivered by means of a small dart, which was found in the eye of the victim. The examination of the last image recorded in his retina showed that the creature, which killed him, looks like this..." A nightmarish monster with four arms, proboscis and tentacles protruding from his belly appeared on the screen. "Some experts say that this is the beginning of the Age of Satan, and that the creature is the Evil itself. They refer to the well-known extraterrestrial message which was engraved on the "watch", recently stolen from the Archaeological Museum, which, as is widely believed, have been delivered from outer space. Ambitious young scientist Diana Pierce has undertaken to track down the monster and lock it up in another "watch", like the first one. That was all in the TMTV news for tonight, ladies and gentlemen. Have a good evening."

Mary switched off her TV-set in exasperation. What a crap! Somebody was probably joking at the expense of the respectable citizens. She pressed the light switch. Nothing happened. So something had, again, gone wrong with this stupid electricity. She felt a surge of anger. She would have to have her supper in the dark again. The last 50 years hadn’t seen much change that way. All the appliances kept misfunctioning as ever. She sat at the table. There was something worrying in the darkness. She was aware of somebody’s presence. And after the hideous monster she had seen on TV... She shuddered. Now she felt as if some unknown creature was lurking behind each piece of furniture, in each dark corner. Then she stood up, deciding that she had definitely seen the shape of something. But in this moment she felt pain in her eye. Impossible. There could be no such thing! A heap of crap! Wild imagination, nothing more! She was tired and it was playing tricks on her...

She couldn’t finish her thought. The pain was enormous. A few minutes later her soul departed her still convulsing body and set off to the world of which there is no return.

The creature stretched out his proboscis contentedly. The great battle was to begin in a few hours. When this fateful second day was over, he, again, would be able to vanish at his will. He had to conquer this planet. The only battle in which he had no right to disappear in the usual way was still ahead. Those were the Rules of the Game. That was the reason why he used the time until midnight to kill and then dissolve into thin air.

May 24, 2043, 2 AM

Diana gestured to the pilot to circle over the block once more. She thought she had seen something, down, to the south. Of course, she might have imagined it, but still she wanted to be sure. She took pride in her eyesight. It couldn’t possible be a human being - it looked green even in the faint moonlight. Besides... she thought she had seen spikes on its back. The pilot made a vast turn and she saw the strange creature once more. This time she had no doubts whatsoever - this was the Evil. She put on the latest model night-vision glasses. The world didn’t look greenish through them as through the older models, but bright and well lit as it was in the daylight. She turned to where the monster was, but didn’t see it. She lowered the glasses and looked again. It was there. She looked again through the glasses. It disappeared. Lowered them. The creature was definitely there. Something in the optical system prevented her from seeing it. Perhaps the new substance they added to the glass. So she would have to cope with it without this aid, and that would be much more difficult. She might miss the creature and the small bottle contained an amount of liquid for no more than two attempts. If she failed humankind would be dead. A small failure like that would be much too costly. She took a breath and nodded to the pilot. He lowered the bird and almost brushed the monster’s head. Diana tried to pour some liquid on it. Just a little, very little. But something disturbed the bird and that was enough for the liquid to miss the monster by more than a yard. The creature heard the splash and started. Then it went to sniff the small puddle. The smell seemed to be familiar, because the monster darted in the opposite direction at full speed. The bird doubled back and followed. When they were close enough and Diana was getting ready for her second attempt, the monster turned back and hit the bird in the eye. The bird winced from the pain and changed her direction. Diana sat back, feeling nothing. She had failed. She had doomed her fellow human beings to death. She was about to throw away the bottle and the new watch, when she noticed that there were still a few drops inside. There was still some hope. The bird was rapidly losing altitude. The convulsions would begin any moment now. She had a moment, perhaps two, to act. The monster had stopped and watched them, obviously glad to have neutralized them. Now! Diana poured the last droplets, but... nothing happened. She had failed again! No, there was no hope for her or humanity. The bird began wriggling and the two of them, the woman and the pilot, fell off her back. Diana remained prostrate, as she had fallen, until she heard some moaning. She turned to look at the creature. A part of it had already disappeared, the rest was dissolving quickly, as if erased by a giant rubber. Only a small heap of dust-like substance remained in the end. Diana nervously swept the dust into the watch and didn’t stop until she was sure to have removed the last speck from the hideous monster. Then, at last, she sighed in relief. The nightmare was over.

The place was somewhat narrow. But it was always like that. Anyway. He’d have to wait. There were lots of other greedy people on this planet. Besides, it didn’t have to be this planet. Somewhere in the Universe there certainly was a planet where they wouldn’t be able to stop him. Then... the Age of Satan would at last begin.
